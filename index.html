About Vipin Kumar

A teacher your child keeps for years, not for one term.

I started teaching in April 2017, and in that time I've taught 50+ students — most of them not for a quick exam push, but for years at a stretch. A large number of my students join around Class 3 or 4 and stay with me right through to Class 10.

That long-term relationship is the whole point. I'm not learning a new child's gaps every few months — I already know where they struggle, how they think, and what explanation finally makes a concept click for them specifically.

My students have been based in India, the UK, the USA and the Netherlands — many of them children of the Indian diaspora juggling a UK or US school curriculum alongside staying connected to home. I teach Maths and Science across CBSE, ICSE, KS3 and GCSE.

Why parents choose Acadika

Built around how families actually want to be taught.

No learning app to download, no dashboard to figure out. Just a teacher, a live class, and a WhatsApp thread that keeps everyone in sync.

A real interactive whiteboard, every class

I teach live using a digital whiteboard built specifically for the way I explain Maths and Science — not a static screen-share. Your child sees every step worked out in real time, the way they would on paper.

Everything happens on WhatsApp

Class links, reminders, and notes — all sent directly to the parent's WhatsApp. No new app to learn, no separate login for your child to manage.

Small batches, 4–5 students max

Small enough that I notice when a concept hasn't landed, and can slow down for the one student who needs it — without holding the rest of the class back.

Notes after every single class

A PDF of what was taught lands on WhatsApp right after the session — so parents always know exactly what their child covered, and revision is never a scramble.

Built for multi-year continuity

Most students stay with me from Class 3–4 right through to Class 10. I track each child's growth year on year, not just topic by topic.

Comfortable across boards

CBSE, ICSE, KS3 and GCSE — I adapt the explanation style and pacing to whichever syllabus your child is actually being examined on.
